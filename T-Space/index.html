About Us

Mental health is a rising issue in our society. We cannot ignore it but our preceding generation does it quite easily. Many parents ignore their child's mental health while others don't have the money to pay for therapy sessions. On an estimate, a therapy session starts at Rs 500 for a 45 mins. If parents are not supportive, it becomes highly difficult to cope with the stress.

We here at T-Space provide free therapy sessions to teens with professional therapists. There are absolutely no upcoming costs. User identity is completely hidden from other users. Users are addressed by the nickname they pick and no one except our team has the access to their name. Along with therapy, we also provide teens with a space to communicate freely - Stories. This is a space where youth can share their trauma and free their hearts off the burden. We also provide a section to take a look on various mental health problems.

ABOUT INDIAN TEENS

How many times have you wondered if your problems were real? How many times you have been wanting to ask for help but couldn't. Globally, 20% of young people experience mental disorders. In India, only 7.3% of its 365 million youth report such problems. Public stigma associated with mental health problems particularly affects help-seeking among young people. Many times parents diregard the child's concerns saying no such disease exists. Just like relationships, teens also hide their mental health from everyone. This does not excuse the fact that they need help. We believe that our community will only grow if we help each other grow and become stronger- Physically and mentally.

Share your Stories with us...

Many people feel worried and dejected every now and then, but when does it take over their whole lives? Losing a loved one, doing poorly in school or work, being bullied and other hardships might lead a person to feel lonely, scared, or anxious. Some people experience this on an everyday basis, sometimes even or no reason at all. They are nothing to hide away from, these journeys should be shared and celebrated instead. We have a place where you can share your struggle and problems. Sharing your coping methods and solutions can help a lot many people out there who might be going through the same things as you are.
